# Poirot Western Star Tea Spread

## Purpose

Reconstruct the tea spread from *Agatha Christie's Poirot*, "The Adventure of
the Western Star," as a practical home entertaining guide.

This is based on visual identification from the scene where Poirot prepares tea
for Marie Marvelle. It is not a confirmed production prop list.

## Service

Scale: 6 to 8 people.

Serve as an afternoon tea with:

- black tea
- cucumber sandwiches
- egg and cress sandwiches
- smoked salmon sandwiches
- scones with jam and cream
- small chocolate tea cakes
- a large decorated ring cake

Recipe direction:

- Treat the sandwiches as classic British afternoon-tea finger sandwiches:
  crustless, small, and cut into fingers or triangles.
- Treat the large centerpiece as a savarin-inspired ring cake. A true savarin is
  yeast-raised and syrup-soaked; a bought bundt-style sponge is acceptable if
  the goal is the visual table effect.
- Treat the dark tiered-stand cakes as small chocolate fairy cakes, mini
  chocolate cakes, or cut squares from a chocolate sheet cake.

Optional additions if the table needs more abundance:

- butter biscuits or shortbread
- grapes or berries for garnish
- lemon slices
- sparkling water

## Prep Plan

### Day Before

- Bake or buy the ring cake.
- Bake or buy the small chocolate tea cakes.
- Boil eggs for egg and cress filling.
- Set out serving plates, tiered stands, teapot, cups, milk jug, sugar bowl,
  napkins, and serving tongs.

### Morning Of

- Make sandwich fillings.
- Slice bread and assemble sandwiches.
- Cover sandwiches with a barely damp tea towel and refrigerate.
- Whip cream if not using clotted cream.
- Glaze and decorate the ring cake.

### 30 Minutes Before

- Plate sandwiches on a tiered stand.
- Plate small cakes on a separate stand.
- Put the ring cake on the table as the visual centerpiece.
- Warm the teapot.
- Boil fresh water shortly before guests arrive.

## Recipes

### Cucumber Finger Sandwiches

Ingredients:

- 1 English cucumber
- 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
- 1 teaspoon lemon juice
- salt
- white pepper
- thin-sliced white bread

Method:

1. Peel the cucumber in stripes if desired, then slice it very thin.
2. Salt the cucumber lightly and let it drain on paper towels for 15 minutes.
3. Mix cream cheese, butter, lemon juice, salt, and white pepper.
4. Spread the mixture thinly on bread.
5. Add cucumber slices in a single layer.
6. Trim crusts and cut into fingers or triangles.

### Egg and Cress Sandwiches

Ingredients:

- 6 hard-boiled eggs
- 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 bunch cress or watercress, chopped
- salt
- black pepper
- thin-sliced white or brown bread

Method:

1. Mash the eggs with mayonnaise and mustard.
2. Fold in cress.
3. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Spread on bread, trim crusts, and cut into triangles.

### Smoked Salmon Sandwiches

Ingredients:

- 8 ounces smoked salmon
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
- 1 teaspoon lemon zest
- 1 teaspoon lemon juice
- black pepper
- thin-sliced brown or wholemeal bread

Method:

1. Mix butter with lemon zest, lemon juice, and black pepper.
2. Spread thinly on bread.
3. Add smoked salmon in an even layer.
4. Trim crusts and cut into fingers.

### Fast Scones

Reference style: classic scones with jam and clotted cream.

Ingredients:

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cubed
- 2/3 cup milk or cream
- 1 egg, beaten, for brushing

Method:

1. Heat oven to 425 F.
2. Whisk flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
3. Rub in cold butter until the mixture looks like coarse crumbs.
4. Stir in milk or cream just until a dough forms.
5. Pat to about 3/4 inch thick and cut rounds.
6. Brush with beaten egg.
7. Bake 12 to 15 minutes, until risen and lightly golden.
8. Serve with jam and cream.

### Small Chocolate Tea Cakes

Use these for the dark cakes visible on the smaller tiered stand.

Reference style: chocolate fairy cakes or mini chocolate cakes.

Ingredients:

- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup cocoa powder
- 3/4 cup sugar
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1/3 cup melted butter
- 2 eggs
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 4 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, melted

Method:

1. Heat oven to 350 F.
2. Grease a mini muffin pan or small square cake pan.
3. Whisk flour, cocoa, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
4. Add milk, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla.
5. Mix until smooth.
6. Bake mini cakes 10 to 14 minutes, or a small sheet 18 to 22 minutes.
7. Cool completely.
8. Drizzle or glaze with melted chocolate.
9. Cut into small squares if baked as a sheet.

### Decorated Ring Cake

The centerpiece in the scene reads as a large ring-shaped tea cake, close to a
savarin, baba, or dressed bundt cake.

Reference style: savarin, a ring-shaped cake served with syrup, cream, and
fruit.

Ingredients:

- 1 large plain ring cake, savarin, baba, or bundt-style sponge
- 1/2 cup apricot jam
- 1 tablespoon water
- berries or grapes
- confectioners' sugar
- whipped cream, optional

Method:

1. Warm apricot jam with water until loose, then strain if needed.
2. Brush the glaze over the ring cake.
3. Fill the center with berries, grapes, or whipped cream.
4. Dust lightly with confectioners' sugar.
5. Serve as the table centerpiece.

## Plating Notes

- Put sandwiches on the tallest stand, arranged by type.
- Put small chocolate cakes on a lower three-tier stand.
- Place the ring cake on its own plate near the front of the table.
- Use white napkins or doilies under pastries to match the scene's linen-heavy
  look.
- Keep the tea service tidy and symmetrical; Poirot would notice.
